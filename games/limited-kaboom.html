Limited Kaboom

Puzzle ★★★★☆
Share:

Game Description

Limited Kaboom is a challenging puzzle game that tests your strategic thinking and resource management skills. In this explosive brain-teaser, you're given a limited number of bombs to clear obstacles and reach objectives across increasingly complex levels. The game's premise is simple but deceptively deep: each level presents you with a grid-based environment filled with various objects - some destructible, some indestructible, and some with special properties. Your goal is to strategically place your limited supply of bombs to clear a path, collect keys, activate switches, and ultimately reach the exit. What makes Limited Kaboom particularly engaging is the careful planning required - bombs have different blast radiuses and effects, and using them inefficiently will leave you without enough explosives to complete the level. As you progress, new mechanics are introduced, such as chain reactions, movable objects, teleporters, and time-sensitive challenges. With its clean, colorful visuals, intuitive controls, and progressively challenging level design, Limited Kaboom offers a perfect blend of casual accessibility and deep puzzle-solving that will keep you thinking "just one more level" as you strategize the perfect explosive solution.

Game Features:

  • 100+ challenging levels with increasing complexity
  • Various bomb types with different blast patterns and effects
  • Strategic resource management with limited explosives
  • Multiple environmental elements that interact with explosions
  • Clean, colorful graphics and satisfying explosion effects
  • Level editor to create and share your own explosive challenges

How to Play

Limited Kaboom features intuitive controls that are easy to learn but offer depth for strategic planning. The game is played primarily with your mouse (or touch controls on mobile devices). To place a bomb, simply click on the desired location on the game grid. Each level provides you with a specific number of bombs, displayed at the top of the screen. Plan carefully, as running out of bombs before completing the objective will require you to restart the level. Different types of bombs become available as you progress through the game. The standard bomb has a cross-shaped blast radius, affecting tiles horizontally and vertically from the explosion point. Later levels introduce specialized explosives like line bombs (which blast in a straight line in one direction), cluster bombs (which create multiple smaller explosions), and remote bombs (which you can detonate at a specific time). The game environment contains various elements that interact differently with explosions: wooden crates can be destroyed with a single bomb, stone walls require multiple explosions to break through, metal barriers are indestructible, and special crystal blocks may reflect or channel explosion forces in different directions. Some levels include collectible items like keys that must be gathered to unlock doors, pressure plates that activate mechanisms when objects are placed on them, and teleporters that transport bombs or other objects to different parts of the level. The game also features chain reaction mechanics - exploding certain objects might trigger secondary explosions or effects, which can be used to your advantage to conserve bombs. If you get stuck on a particularly challenging level, you can use the hint button (lightbulb icon) for a suggestion, though this is limited to prevent overreliance. For the most efficient solutions, take time to observe the level layout before placing any bombs, and consider how explosions might interact with different elements. Remember that the order and placement of your bombs often matters as much as which objects you choose to destroy. With practice and strategic thinking, you'll master the art of controlled demolition and solve even the most complex explosive puzzles!

Limited Kaboom has completely taken over my lunch breaks for the past two weeks! The difficulty curve is perfectly balanced - each new mechanic is introduced gradually, giving you time to understand it before the game combines it with other elements in increasingly complex ways. I'm currently stuck on level 47 (the one with the alternating teleporters and ice blocks), and I keep coming back to it because I know the solution is just one insight away. What I particularly appreciate about this game is that there's usually more than one way to solve each puzzle. My friend and I compared our solutions for the crystal cavern levels, and we had completely different approaches that both worked! For anyone struggling with the early levels, here's a tip that helped me: always look for chain reaction opportunities before placing your first bomb. Sometimes destroying one key object can trigger a cascade that clears half the level. The special bombs introduced around level 30 add another layer of strategy - the directional bombs are particularly useful for precise targeting without wasting explosive power. I also love the little details, like how different materials have unique destruction animations and sound effects. The level editor is a fantastic addition too - I've created a few challenges for my friends that have them completely stumped! My only minor criticism would be that some of the later levels can feel a bit trial-and-error, but the quick restart option makes this less frustrating than it could be. Overall, this is easily one of the most engaging puzzle games I've played this year. Highly recommended if you enjoy strategic thinking and resource management challenges!
