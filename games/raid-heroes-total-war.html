Raid Heroes: Total War

Strategy ★★★★★
Share:

Game Description

Raid Heroes: Total War is an epic strategy game that combines kingdom building, hero collection, and tactical combat in a rich fantasy world teeming with opportunity and danger. Set in a vast medieval realm where various factions vie for supremacy, players take on the role of a fledgling lord tasked with establishing and expanding their domain while defending against rival powers and monstrous threats. The game features a compelling blend of macro and micro management, allowing players to oversee the development of their kingdom while also commanding heroes and armies in tactical battles. At its core, Raid Heroes: Total War revolves around collecting and developing a diverse roster of legendary heroes, each with unique abilities, specializations, and backstories. These champions can be deployed in various combat scenarios, from defending your territory against invaders to leading offensive campaigns against enemy strongholds. Players must strategically assemble teams of complementary heroes, considering faction synergies, ability combinations, and counter-strategies against different opponents. The kingdom building aspect provides depth beyond combat, with players constructing and upgrading various structures to generate resources, research technologies, train troops, and unlock new hero capabilities. Resource management and strategic development choices create meaningful progression paths that influence your military strength and economic prosperity. What sets Raid Heroes: Total War apart is its emphasis on tactical depth in combat. Battles play out on grid-based maps where positioning, terrain advantages, and ability timing are crucial for victory. Players must consider unit types, elemental affinities, and special abilities when formulating battle strategies, creating a chess-like layer of tactical decision-making. The game features multiple PvE campaign modes with story-driven missions, challenging boss encounters, and special events, as well as PvP arenas where players can test their strategies against other lords. A robust alliance system allows players to join forces with others, participate in alliance wars, and tackle cooperative challenges that would be impossible to overcome alone. With its stunning fantasy visuals, strategic depth, extensive hero collection system, and both competitive and cooperative gameplay options, Raid Heroes: Total War offers an immersive strategy experience that rewards thoughtful planning, tactical execution, and long-term progression.

Game Features:

  • Extensive hero collection system with unique abilities and progression paths

  • Tactical grid-based combat with emphasis on positioning and strategy
  • PvE campaigns with story-driven missions and challenging boss encounters
  • Competitive PvP arenas and alliance-based warfare
  • Regular events and updates with new content and challenges

How to Play

Raid Heroes: Total War features intuitive controls designed to make complex strategy accessible while still offering depth for experienced players. On desktop, you'll primarily use the following controls: Mouse for selecting heroes, buildings, and targets. Left-click to select units, confirm actions, and navigate menus. Right-click to view detailed information about heroes, abilities, or buildings. Number keys (1-5) for quickly activating hero abilities during combat. Tab key to cycle between different game sections. Spacebar to pause/unpause during battles. On mobile devices, the touch controls are adapted for smaller screens: Tap on heroes, buildings, or enemies to select them. Drag and drop to move heroes on the battlefield or rearrange team formations. Tap ability icons to activate hero skills during combat. Pinch to zoom in and out of the kingdom view. When you first start the game, you'll go through a tutorial that introduces the basic mechanics of kingdom management and combat. This includes: How to construct and upgrade buildings in your kingdom. How to summon, level up, and equip heroes. How to form effective teams and engage in combat. How to join an alliance and participate in cooperative activities. The main game modes include: Campaign Mode takes you through the main storyline, with progressively challenging stages and boss encounters. Dungeon Raids offer specialized challenges with unique rewards for different hero types. Arena allows you to compete against other players' teams in PvP combat. Alliance War pits your alliance against others in large-scale territorial conflicts. Event Towers provide limited-time challenges with special rewards. Kingdom management is a key aspect of the game: The Castle is your main structure, determining your overall kingdom level and unlocking new features as it's upgraded. Barracks train various troop types that support your heroes in battle. The Academy researches technologies that provide kingdom-wide bonuses. Resource buildings (farms, mines, etc.) generate essential materials for construction and upgrades. The Summoning Portal is where you acquire new heroes through various recruitment methods. Hero management involves several systems: Heroes are classified by rarity (from Common to Legendary) and faction (such as Kingdom, Forest, Undead, etc.). Each hero has a unique set of abilities that can be upgraded using skill points. Equipment can be collected and equipped to enhance hero stats and provide special effects. Hero ascension allows you to increase a hero's maximum level and unlock powerful passive abilities. Team formation is crucial for success: Most combat scenarios allow you to deploy 5 heroes in various formations. Position tanks in front to absorb damage while placing damage dealers and support heroes in safer positions. Consider faction bonuses that activate when using multiple heroes from the same group. Create synergistic teams where heroes' abilities complement each other. Combat follows a turn-based system with active time elements: Heroes take turns based on their speed stat, with faster heroes acting more frequently. Each hero has basic attacks and special abilities with cooldowns. Positioning is crucial - some abilities affect areas, making placement strategic. The battlefield contains various terrain features that can provide advantages or create obstacles. For beginners, here are some tips to get started: Focus on a core team of 5 heroes rather than spreading resources too thinly across many characters. Complete daily quests and missions for essential resources and progression materials. Join an active alliance as soon as possible to access additional rewards and assistance. Save premium currency (gems) for strategic summons during rate-up events. As you become more experienced, you can explore more advanced strategies: Counter-building teams specifically to defeat challenging content or particular enemy compositions. Optimizing gear sets to maximize specific hero roles (damage, tanking, support, etc.). Timing ability usage to create powerful combo effects between different heroes. The game includes various progression systems beyond just leveling heroes: Guild Tech research provides permanent stat boosts to all your heroes of specific classes. The Great Hall offers faction-specific enhancements that strengthen all heroes from that faction. Collections reward you for acquiring specific sets of heroes or items. Remember that Raid Heroes: Total War is a game of strategic choices and long-term progression. Building a powerful kingdom and hero roster takes time, but thoughtful resource management and tactical combat prowess will lead you to victory!

StrategyMaster 2024-08-23

As someone who's played countless strategy games over the years, I'm impressed by how Raid Heroes: Total War manages to balance accessibility with genuine strategic depth. The game onboards new players effectively while still offering enough complexity to keep veterans engaged. What sets this game apart from similar titles is the tactical combat system. Positioning your heroes correctly, timing ability usage, and understanding terrain advantages creates a layer of strategy that goes beyond simply having the highest-level characters. I've won battles against teams with significantly higher power ratings through superior tactics, which feels incredibly rewarding. The hero collection aspect is well-implemented, with each character feeling distinct rather than just being stat variations. I particularly appreciate how even lower-rarity heroes can remain viable throughout the game when properly built and supported by the right team composition. This makes the gacha system feel less punishing than in many similar games. Kingdom management provides a nice complementary gameplay loop to the combat. The building choices feel meaningful, with clear tradeoffs between focusing on resource generation, military strength, or hero development. I've experimented with different kingdom development strategies across multiple accounts and found several viable approaches depending on your playstyle. For new players, I recommend focusing on a core team of 5 heroes from no more than 2 factions to maximize synergy bonuses. The Warrior/Mage combination has worked particularly well for me in the early game, with Warriors providing frontline protection while Mages deliver powerful area damage. Also, don't neglect your daily quests and alliance activities - these provide essential resources that accelerate your progress significantly. The alliance system deserves special mention for how it encourages cooperation without forcing constant participation. The shared rewards and assistance mechanics create a genuine sense of community, and the alliance wars provide some of the most exciting large-scale strategic challenges in the game. If I could suggest one improvement, it would be adding more variety to the endgame PvE content. The current challenge towers and boss raids are fun, but they can become somewhat formulaic once you've mastered them. Adding procedurally generated challenges or more dynamic encounters would help maintain engagement for veteran players. Overall, Raid Heroes: Total War has become my go-to strategy game for both quick sessions and deeper strategic planning. The combination of hero collection, tactical combat, and kingdom building creates a compelling package that strategy fans shouldn't miss.

CasualTactician 2024-08-22

I downloaded Raid Heroes: Total War expecting another generic hero collector, but I've been pleasantly surprised by how engaging and strategic the gameplay actually is. As someone who doesn't have hours to spend gaming each day, I appreciate how the game respects my time while still offering meaningful progression. The auto-battle feature is particularly well-implemented - it's useful for grinding easier content, but the AI isn't smart enough for challenging stages, creating a natural incentive to take manual control when strategy really matters. What I enjoy most is the variety of viable team compositions. Rather than having a single meta team that everyone uses, the game encourages experimentation with different hero combinations and strategies. I've found success with an unconventional poison-focused team that many players overlook, which makes victories feel like personal achievements rather than just following a guide. The visuals deserve special mention - the character designs are distinctive and full of personality, and the battle animations are satisfying without being so lengthy that they slow down gameplay. I particularly appreciate the attention to detail in how different abilities are visualized, making it easy to understand what's happening in complex battle situations. For other casual players considering this game, I recommend joining an active alliance as soon as possible. The shared resources and advice from more experienced players accelerated my progress significantly. Don't worry about not being able to contribute much initially - most alliances are happy to help newcomers grow. The events are generally well-balanced for different player levels, with rewards that feel worthwhile without requiring obsessive grinding. I can usually complete the important parts of most events with about 30-45 minutes of play per day, which fits perfectly into my schedule. My only criticism would be that the tutorial could do a better job explaining some of the more nuanced systems like gear set bonuses and faction synergies. I had to learn these through trial and error or by asking alliance members, which might frustrate some newer players. Overall, Raid Heroes: Total War has found that sweet spot of strategic depth without overwhelming complexity that makes it perfect for players like me who enjoy thoughtful gameplay but don't have endless hours to invest. It's become my go-to game when I want something more substantial than a casual mobile game but can't commit to a full PC strategy session.
